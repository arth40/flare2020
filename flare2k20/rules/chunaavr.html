CHUNAAV

REGISTRATION FEES: 50 INR per head.

The objective of the event is to elect a party to form the government of a fictional country. The event will be a simulation of an election campaign for a fictional country where participants will be required to form their own parties, debate on certain key issues and spread their ideologies, culminating in a popular vote to decide the best party. The party members will also be required to campaign, mobilize people and garner more voters through their creativity, charisma and persuasion. The event will begin with an introductory session taken by an EC member on election campaigns, followed by debate/discussion, campaigning and culminating with the popular vote.

RULES:

  • You can either participate as member of a party or an independent candidate.
  • To form a party minimum 3 people are required. There is no limit for maximum no. of people in a party. You cannot choose existing parties.
  • Once the party is formed, you will have to assign your party a name, symbol and slogan, along with the ideology and vision, thus forming the manifesto.
  • A leader of party has to be chosen among the members.
  • There will be relevant questions thrown on the floor and one representative from each party will be required to answer. A panel of 3 judges will decide the best speaker among all the participants.
  • Following the debate/discussion, campaigning will commence where each party will be given a period of 2 hours to roam around the campus and mobilize as many people as possible to vote for them. They can be as creative as possible to convince people to come and vote.
  • After the 2-hour period ends, a one-hour window will be open for everyone to cast their vote. Everyone with a verified college ID card will be allowed to vote.
  • The party with the maximum votes will be declared the winner of the event and rewarded with a cash prize.
  • The party with the most creative name, logo and slogan will also be rewarded.
  • A candidate can use the following tools during the discussion-
    • Point of information- If a candidate feels wrong information has been used during the discussion then he/she can raise 'point of information' to judges in order to rectify the information.
    • Point of enquiry- If a candidate has any query regarding the procedure then the candidate can raise 'point of enquiry' to clear his/her doubt.
    • Point of personal privilege- If a candidate needs any assistance like water or tea then he/she can raise 'point of privilege'.
    • Point for defect- If a candidate wants to defect to another party during the discussion then he/she can with the permission of the party he/she wishes to join. A person can also leave a party to become an independent candidate.
